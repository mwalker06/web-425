Ludwig van Beethoven



Ludwig van Beethoven, born in 1770, was a highly esteemed German composer and pianist. Considered a towering figure in the history of music, Beethoven's impact on the Classical and Romantic periods remains profound. Originally hailing from Bonn, he later ventured to Vienna, where he received tutelage from the renowned composer Joseph Haydn. Despite experiencing deafness, Beethoven continued to compose remarkable works that pushed the boundaries of symphonies, chamber music, and piano compositions. Famous masterpieces include his iconic Ninth Symphony and "Moonlight Sonata." Beethoven's enduring legacy lies in his innovative compositions, which continue to captivate and inspire audiences worldwide.
